Honey is a sweet, viscous, semi-translucent liquid that honeybees make from the nectar of flowering plants. It can range in color from pale yellow or yellowish brown to dark brown, and has a sweet, aromatic odor and a sweet, acrid taste. The flavor and color of honey are determined by the flowers from which the nectar is gathered. For example, some of the most commercially desirable honeys come from clover.

Honey is a versatile and natural substance with various uses. It serves as a natural sweetener in food and beverages, and its antibacterial and antifungal properties make it a popular ingredient in health remedies, skincare products, and wound care treatments. Honey is also used as a natural energy source in sports and fitness, and has medicinal uses in treating allergies, arthritis, and digestive issues.
